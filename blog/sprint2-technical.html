Inline vs Inline-block

The 'display: inline' and 'display: inline-block' functions are used to, well, pretty much display items on the page.

The 'display: inline' element will display the element side to side like so:

A little kitten and puppy went to the supermarket and bought canned tuna for their butler, Human.

This could be a problem if you wanted to add a specified width and a height to the elements.

This is where the 'display: inline-block' value comes into the picture:

A little kitten and puppy went to the supermarket and bought canned tuna for their butler, Human.

'display: inline-block' allows a width and height to be added into the element.

It also respects the top and bottom margin/paddings, which the 'display: inline' does not do.
